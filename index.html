I am an aspiring developer, currently living in Padua (but open to relocate) and I'm 31 years old.
I've always loved computers. My father used to work in a computer shop (Mr. Robot, anyone?) and since I was a child, I've always been surrounded by computers. I mostly used them to play videogames, but when I was old enough to start High School, I chose informatics, to take a step further into my passion. Unfortunately during High School I found out I'm not very good with mathematics and physics, so when the time came to choose a career path, I went to study Psychology at University of Padua.
I started working in Human Resources in 2013, but something kept dragging me towards Informatics. In fact, in the year 2017 I moved to Padua as I was chosen to start up the North-Eastern Italy branch of Randstad Technologies, a division whose aim is to recruit IT professionals. The more I worked with them, spoke with them and got to know them, the more I understood that my place should have been on the other side of the fence. So here I am!

Goals

Sitting in front of a computer is one of the things I love the most. Until now, though, my job has mostly been focused on keeping relations with different stalkeholders.
In my professional future, I'd like to be able to match my capability in interacting with people with a wider knownledge in terms of technical skills. For this reason, I see myself in a role which has a lot of development stuff to do, as well as a direct contact with the client when it comes to gather requisitions and user expectations.
I see myself as a good fit in both a big company or a small firm. I believe I could provide value in terms of soft skills, other than hard technical knownledge. In terms of technical expectations, in the future I'd like to focus more on web applications and software development. I'm not sure if I have enough aesthetic taste to work as a front-end developer ;)
